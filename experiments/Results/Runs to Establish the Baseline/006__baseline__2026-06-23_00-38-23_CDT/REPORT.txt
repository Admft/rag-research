WHY IT SCORED LOWER (despite better retrieval)
----------------------------------------
1. Citation accuracy (−28 pp) — largest driver. 19/60 questions scored
   0% citation vs 1/60 in 005. JSON answers often omit [Doc X] tags or
   use variants like "[Document ID: 2]" that the citation parser does not
   recognize. This alone costs ~2.8 points on the weighted final score.

2. Context recall (−11 pp) — top_sentences_5 strips each chunk to five
   sentences before generation and judging. The judge sees ~⅓ the context
   (avg prompt ~506 words vs ~1471 in 005), so it more often rates
   retrieved context as insufficient even when Recall@5 is perfect.

3. Answer correctness (−7 pp) — shorter filtered context plus briefer JSON
   answers from qwen2.5:14b produce less overlap with hand-written
   expected answers. Biggest per-question drops: enhancingrag_q5 (−65.5),
   rgb_q3 (−50.8), queryrewrite_q1 (−44.5).

4. Not a retrieval regression — Recall@5 improved to 100% (selfrag_q1 now
   rank 1 via hybrid + BM25 normalization). MRR held steady. The final
   score drop is almost entirely generation, citation format, and context
   filtering — not fetch quality.
